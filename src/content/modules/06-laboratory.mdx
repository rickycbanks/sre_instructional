# Module 6: Practice like an operator, not just like a reader

At this point in the path, the biggest risk is staying in "I understand the article" mode. Strong operational practice depends on people who can:

- reason out loud
- prioritize under uncertainty
- explain tradeoffs cleanly
- connect operational choices to customer impact

## A simple capstone that creates real operating material

Build a small service and take it through a reliability loop:

1. deploy it somewhere you can operate repeatedly
2. add health checks and metrics
3. define one availability and one latency SLO
4. create at least one actionable alert
5. force a failure and document the response
6. write a short blameless postmortem

Even a modest project creates far better operational stories than only reading theory.

## What to rehearse out loud

Take any scenario and practice this order:

1. restate the problem in user terms
2. identify the most important signals
3. pick the safest next action
4. explain the tradeoff
5. describe the preventive follow-up

## How to turn practice into better explanations

- keep a short library of incidents, tradeoffs, and automation wins
- measure yourself on structure, not just correctness
- revisit weak competencies in the progress dashboard instead of repeating your favorite topics
